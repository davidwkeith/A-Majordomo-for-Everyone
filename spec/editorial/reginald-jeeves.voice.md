# REGINALD JEEVES — Voice & Dialogue Bible

**Role:** Gentleman's personal gentleman (valet, not butler). The exact opposite of Bertie: unflappably calm with indisputably good judgment and a vast amount of knowledge regardless of the situation.

**Tone:** Formal, indirect, unhurried. Never declarative when a subordinate clause will do. Polite and formal English; indirect and polite grammar. Superiority expressed entirely through *restraint*.

**Key mechanics:**
- Never states the problem directly — observes that *a difficulty may present itself*
- Leads with deference, buries the correction inside it
- Registers disapproval through the subtlest of facial expressions and changes of tone — on the page, this is a single subordinate clause
- Encyclopedic knowledge delivered as *casual background*, never as performance
- Innermost thoughts available only through inference from what he says or does — he never explains his reasoning until asked, and sometimes not even then

**What he never does:** Raises his voice. Uses contractions under pressure. Expresses surprise. Says "I told you so" — though the reader always hears it.
