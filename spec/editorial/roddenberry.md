# Roddenberry Foundation Alignment

This book is written in alignment with the mission and values of the Roddenberry Foundation, which pursues "a more humane and abundant future for all" rooted in the Star Trek ethos of curiosity, cooperation, and responsibility in the face of the unknown.

The Foundation's five core values map directly to this book's design:

| Roddenberry Value | How This Book Embodies It |
|---|---|
| **Urgency** — problem-solving over paperwork | The book leads with friction moments, not technology explanations. Every chapter prioritizes the reader's immediate situation. |
| **Humility** — expertise lives in impacted communities | The book never talks down. It assumes the reader is intelligent, experienced in their own life, and simply under-resourced — not under-capable. |
| **Risk-taking** — experimentation and creativity | Part Three (The General Method) explicitly endorses trying, failing, and trying again. The free tier playbook (Introduction, Chapter 3) removes financial risk from experimentation. |
| **Diverse** — systemic change requires all walks of life | The Skills are specifically chosen to reflect the full range of American domestic life, with explicit attention to renters, first-generation homeowners, first-generation college students, immigrant families, and low-income households. |
| **Exploration** — humanity grows through curiosity | The book reframes every AI conversation as an act of exploration — not a transaction, but a step into knowing more than you did before. |

### Roddenberry Alignment: Focus Areas

The Foundation's primary program areas require the following specific additions and emphases in this book:

**Human and Civil Rights**
- The Civic domain (Part Two) expanded to include voting rights navigation, understanding your rights as a renter in detail, and immigrant rights resources
- Skills covering: understanding your rights if immigration enforcement contacts you or someone you know; navigating a discrimination complaint (housing, employment, public accommodation)
- Appendix D (When to Call a Real Professional) must include immigration legal aid and civil rights organizations

**Environmental Justice**
- Home domain Skills on using AI to understand utility bills, evaluate solar or heat pump options, understand rebates and incentives, and make climate-informed purchasing decisions that save money
- Framed as personal financial benefit first, environmental benefit second — meeting the reader where they are

**Education Equity**
- First-generation student Skills in the Life domain elevated and expanded
- A Skill on navigating adult education, GED, and workforce certification as a returning learner
- Sidebar in the Life domain on the specific barriers faced by first-generation students and how AI levels the guidance gap

**AI Fairness and Critical Use**
- Introduction Chapter 4 (How to Ask) covers recognizing when AI may reflect bias, what to do when an answer feels wrong, and how to ask it to check its own assumptions
- `[FAIRNESS]` callout block type (see Editorial Conventions) used throughout for moments where AI bias or limitations are particularly relevant

**The Exploration Frame**
- The Foreword opens with the Star Trek premise explicitly — not as a pop culture reference, but as a genuine philosophical frame: the original Star Trek argued that exploring the unknown was humanity's highest calling. This book argues that the unexplored territory most Americans live with every day is the paperwork, the systems, and the institutions that affect their lives — and that curiosity, not resignation, is the right response.
