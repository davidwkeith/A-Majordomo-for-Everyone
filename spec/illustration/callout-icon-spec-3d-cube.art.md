The isometric 3D cube. Three visible faces drawn in perspective — top, left, right — the doodle every kid drew when they learned that two diagonal lines and a few parallels make a box look real. Precise parallel lines, consistent angles, maybe a faint vanishing-point construction line visible beneath. The top face slightly lighter pressure, the right face shaded with light crosshatching to give it depth. Technical, structural, a shape defined by its specification. No color — just graphite. Margin-sized, roughly 1cm tall. The same hand that drew the Trinitron.
