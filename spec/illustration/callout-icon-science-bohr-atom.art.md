Callout Icon: The Bohr atom doodle. A small solid-filled circle for the nucleus, three elliptical orbits at different angles crossing around it, a small dot on each orbit for the electron. The doodle version, not the accurate version — orbits too circular, electrons too big, the whole thing more logo than physics. Drawn the way every kid drew it on their science textbook cover in 7th grade. Careful ellipses with maybe a slight compass-wobble. No color, no shading — just graphite. Margin-sized, roughly 1cm tall. The same hand that drew the Trinitron.

**Background: pure white, `#FFFFFF`, flat.** Not gray, not off-white, not cream, not paper texture, not a card, not a notebook page. Just the pencil drawing on a clean white field. The build removes white to create transparency, so any gray will show as a halo in the ePub.
