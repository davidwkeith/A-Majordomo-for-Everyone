Callout Icon: The yin-yang. A circle with the S-curve down the middle dividing it into two halves, one filled with pencil shading, one left white, a small dot in each half (white dot in the dark half, filled dot in the light half). The circle drawn carefully, maybe traced around a coin — nearly perfect but with a faint wobble that says freehand. The S-curve centered, the dots placed precisely. The shaded half built up in directional pencil strokes, not smudged. Every kid in the 90s drew one of these on a binder. No color — just graphite. Margin-sized, roughly 1cm tall. The same hand that drew the Trinitron.

**Background: pure white, `#FFFFFF`, flat.** Not gray, not off-white, not cream, not paper texture, not a card, not a notebook page. Just the pencil drawing on a clean white field — including the unshaded half of the yin-yang, which must be the same pure white as the surround. The build removes white to create transparency, so any gray will show as a halo in the ePub.
