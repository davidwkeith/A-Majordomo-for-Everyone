#2 pencil sketch of an original Apple iPad (2010, first generation),
held in **portrait orientation**.

**Orientation and proportions --- non-negotiable:**

- **Portrait, not landscape.** The iPad is taller than it is wide.
- **Home button on the bottom short edge,** centered. A single round
  recessed button. Not on the side. Not on the long edge.
- **Front-facing camera dot on the top short edge,** centered.
- **Aspect ratio** of the overall device approximately matches a
  sheet of US Letter paper (8.5x11) --- about 1:1.29, height to width.
  Noticeably taller than wide. Not square. Not 4:3. Not landscape.
- Thick aluminum bezel around the screen. Rounded corners on the
  outer body.

If the iPad ends up square, or wider than tall, or with the home
button on the long edge, the image is wrong.

**On the screen,** in 8-bit pixel art: Roy and Moss from *The IT
Crowd*, side by side. Roy --- brown skin, dark curly hair, white
short-sleeved shirt, necktie, holding a phone receiver to his ear
with a frustrated expression. Moss --- pale skin, brown bowl-cut hair,
large square glasses, brown corduroy jacket, holding a red-and-green
fire extinguisher. Both characters approximately 32x48 pixels,
rendered side by side, filling the screen vertically. 4-color palette
per character.

**True pixel grid --- non-negotiable.** The figures on the screen are
actual 8-bit pixel art, not blocky illustration. Render on a literal
uniform grid of axis-aligned square pixels --- as if captured from an
NES framebuffer and enlarged with nearest-neighbor scaling. Every
pixel the same size, every pixel a single flat color. Hard edges, no
anti-aliasing, no smooth curves, no airbrush, no pencil shading or
hatching inside the pixel figures. The viewer should be able to count
individual pixels along an edge.

**The screen background is solid (plain black or plain white).** No
graph paper, no grid lines, no ruled lines, no notebook page showing
through the screen. The iPad's screen is a screen, not a sheet of
paper.

**No meta-elements.** No "The IT Crowd" title text on the screen, no
caption box, no logo, no labels, no sidebar, no UI chrome, no app
icons, no status bar. No furniture or scenery behind the iPad. Just
the iPad and the two characters on its screen.

The iPad frame and bezel are #2 pencil on pure white background ---
same hand as the rest of the book, visible pencil strokes, slight
wobble, erasure ghosts. The pixel art on the screen is colored in
ballpoint pen. The whole thing looks like a kid drew their favorite
show on the exciting new device, which is exactly what happened.
