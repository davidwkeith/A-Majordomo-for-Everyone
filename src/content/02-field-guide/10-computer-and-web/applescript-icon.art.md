The original AppleScript icon from the early '90s: a paper scroll, slightly curled at top and bottom, with a tiny cursor arrow and a zigzag line of "code" drawn on it. 32x32 pixels scaled up. Rendered in the flat 1-bit System 7 style. Sits on the standard white icon square with drop shadow. The first programming language designed to read like English — tell application "Finder" to open folder. Vibe coding before there were vibes.
