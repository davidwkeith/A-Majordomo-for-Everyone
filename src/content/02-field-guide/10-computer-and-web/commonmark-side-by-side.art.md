Hand-drawn #2 pencil split-panel illustration. Two side-by-side
panes separated by a thin vertical pencil rule down the middle of
the frame. Both panes sit on the same pure white background; there is
no outer box around the whole illustration.

**Left pane --- the source.** A small page of plain Markdown text
rendered in a hand-drawn monospaced look (each character in its own
neat box, slight wobble, the way a Gen-X kid would draw "computer
text"). The content is a fictional, illustrative snippet
approximating John Gruber's original Markdown introduction --- a
heading line beginning with `#`, a paragraph with `*emphasis*` and
`**strong**` markers, a short bulleted list with `-` bullets, and a
`[link text](url)` line. The exact words are placeholder, not a
verbatim quote.

**Right pane --- the rendered view.** The same content rendered as
it would appear in a browser: a real heading, a paragraph with
italic and bold runs, a bulleted list with round bullets, and a
hyperlink in blue with an underline. No browser chrome, no URL bar,
no window frame --- just the rendered text floating on white.

A small pencil arrow may cross the dividing rule, source-side to
rendered-side, suggesting the transformation. No labels.

**No meta-elements --- non-negotiable.** The illustration contains
only the two panes of text. No color swatches, palettes, legends,
keys, hex codes, "BEFORE / AFTER" labels, sidebar text, or any UI
explaining the colors or technique. No CommonMark or Markdown
wordmark or logo.

**Background: pure white, `#FFFFFF`, flat.** Not gray, not off-white,
not cream, not paper texture, not a notebook page, not the original
"light gray code block" treatment. The build removes white to create
transparency, so any gray will show as a halo in the ePub. The
source pane is plain white with hand-drawn monospace text on it ---
no shaded code-block background.

**Watch out for:**

- NO browser chrome --- no Chrome/Safari window, no address bar, no
  tabs
- NO verbatim copy of John Gruber's actual Daring Fireball post ---
  use placeholder text in the same shape
- NO graph paper or ruled-paper backdrop in either pane
- NO real URL in the link --- use a clearly placeholder href
