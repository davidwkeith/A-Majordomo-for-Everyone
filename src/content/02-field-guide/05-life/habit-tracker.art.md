Hand-drawn #2 pencil sketch of a single month torn from a wall calendar, the kind record stores sold out of their old LP bins. Clearly a Nirvana concert photo, but most of that's out of frame. The month grid is hand-ruled in pencil — seven columns, five rows, day numbers written small in each cell's upper-right corner. April 1994. A pencil is freshly stuffed into the spiral.

Most days have a bold X drawn through them in pencil — the tracking mark. The X marks vary: some are heavy and confident (good days), some are lighter and quicker (got it done but barely), a couple are traced over twice. Four days across the month have no X at all — visible gaps, blank cells among the marked ones. The gaps are not clustered at the end (the typical "gave up" pattern). They are scattered: day 5, day 14, day 22. Life happened. Kurt died. The habit survived.

The current day the 28th of the month, has a fresh X — the most recent one.

Small detail: in the margin below the grid, a handwritten note too small to read clearly — the kind of annotation someone adds to remind themselves why they started.

Plain white background. The calendar page is a drawn object, not the background — edges visible, slightly curled at the bottom corner. The visual argument: consistency is not perfection. The gaps are the point.
