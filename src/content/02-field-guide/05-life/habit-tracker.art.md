Hand-drawn #2 pencil sketch of a single month torn from a wall
calendar, the kind record stores sold out of their old LP bins.
Clearly a Nirvana concert photo above the grid, but most of that's
out of frame --- a glimpse of stage lighting, the silhouette of a
guitar headstock, no readable band logo. The month grid is hand-ruled
in pencil --- seven columns, five rows, day numbers written small in
each cell's upper-right corner. April 1994. A pencil is freshly
stuffed into the spiral binding at the top.

Most days have a bold X drawn through them in pencil --- the tracking
mark. The X marks vary: some are heavy and confident (good days),
some are lighter and quicker (got it done but barely), a couple are
traced over twice. Four days across the month have no X at all ---
visible gaps, blank cells among the marked ones. The gaps are not
clustered at the end (the typical "gave up" pattern). They are
scattered: day 5, day 14, day 22. Life happened. Kurt died. The
habit survived.

The current day, the 28th, has a fresh X --- the most recent one.

Small detail: in the margin below the grid, a handwritten note too
small to read clearly --- the kind of annotation someone adds to
remind themselves why they started. Squiggle-legible, not a real
quote, not a real name.

The calendar page is a drawn object, not the background --- edges
visible, slightly curled at the bottom corner.

**No meta-elements --- non-negotiable.** The illustration contains
only the calendar page. No color swatches, palettes, legends, keys,
hex codes, callouts from outside the page, sidebar text, or any UI
explaining the colors or technique. No external "HABIT TRACKER"
caption.

**Background: pure white, `#FFFFFF`, flat.** Not gray, not off-white,
not cream, not paper texture, not a wall, not a desk. The calendar
page is the only object; the field around it is pure white. The build
removes white to create transparency, so any gray will show as a halo
in the ePub.

**Watch out for:**

- NO readable Nirvana wordmark, no Kurt Cobain face, no real concert
  photo composition --- the photo above the grid is a suggestion,
  cropped and abstracted
- NO real person's name written into the margin note
- NO motivational quote, no "DON'T BREAK THE CHAIN," no app-style
  streak counter
- NO brand name on the calendar (no Hallmark, no At-A-Glance)
- NO graph paper or ruled-paper backdrop --- the calendar sits on
  pure white
