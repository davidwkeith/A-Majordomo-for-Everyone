### Life

*The systems that govern health, money, and legal standing get their own domains in this guide. What remains is everything else: the conversations, the milestones, the meals, the trips, the slow accumulation of skills and relationships that constitute an actual life. The billionaire class has a staff for this too. Now you have one.*

#### Li-1: Preparing for a Difficult Conversation
**Strategy:** Prepare + Draft
**The Majordomo says:** *Research on interpersonal conflict spanning 40 years points to the same finding: how a difficult conversation begins predicts its outcome with approximately 96% accuracy. The words chosen in the first thirty seconds determine most of what follows. Preparation is not a luxury. It is the intervention.*
**The spec:**
```
I need to have a difficult conversation with [relationship] about [topic].
My goal is [specific outcome -- not "fix everything," something concrete].
I'm worried about [specific thing that could go wrong].
I want to [preserve the relationship / set a clear boundary /
reach a specific agreement -- choose one].
Please help me:
1. Identify the key thing I need to say clearly
2. Anticipate the most likely responses and how to handle them
3. Draft an opening statement I could use to start the conversation
```
**Science note:** Gottman (1999) identifies the "soft startup" as the single highest-leverage intervention in difficult conversations -- how you begin determines nearly everything that follows.[^li1-1]

[^li1-1]: Gottman, J.M. (1999). *The Marriage Clinic.* W.W. Norton.

#### Li-2: Writing a Eulogy
**Strategy:** Draft
**The Majordomo says:** *A eulogy is the one piece of writing in most people's lives for which there is no second draft, no opportunity to revise after seeing the reaction, and no possibility of starting over. It is also written under conditions of maximum emotional impairment, saved online forever. Your Agent can provide the structure. Only you can provide the life.*
**The spec:**
```
I need to write a eulogy for [relationship -- "my father," "my friend"].
Here are the things about them that I most want people to remember:
[list 3-5 specific memories, qualities, or stories]
The tone should be [celebratory / reflective / a mix].
The audience is [family and close friends / a large public service].
I have about [X] minutes.
Please draft a eulogy I can edit into my own voice.
The stories should come from what I've told you -- please don't
invent details I haven't given you.
```

#### Li-3: Recognizing a Scam
**Strategy:** Research + Draft
**See also:** Li-1: Preparing for a Difficult Conversation (for talking to a loved one who has been targeted)
**The Majordomo says:** *Scams work because they exploit responses that are, in every other context, healthy: trust, urgency, deference to authority, desire to help. The person who falls for one was not being stupid. They were being human in a situation engineered by professionals to weaponize that humanity. The FTC reports Americans lost over $10 billion to fraud in 2023. The shame that follows a successful scam suppresses reporting, so the actual number is higher. The shame is the second scam.*
**The spec:**
```
I think I [or someone I care about] may be dealing with a scam.
Here is what happened: [describe the situation — the initial contact,
what was asked for, what was promised, what has been given so far].
Please:
1. Tell me if this matches a known scam pattern and which one
2. Explain what the scam is actually trying to accomplish
3. Tell me what to do RIGHT NOW — what to stop, who to call,
   what to document
4. If money or information has already been sent, what recovery
   options exist
Be direct. I need the honest answer.
```
**What to do with the output:** If you are helping a loved one, lead with concern, not correction. The shame is already doing damage. Your Agent can help you draft that conversation (see Li-1). If you are the one targeted, file with the FTC at [reportfraud.ftc.gov](https://reportfraud.ftc.gov) and your state attorney general. Speed matters — some financial transactions can be reversed within 24-48 hours.

> **[SCIENCE]** Cialdini's six principles of influence (1984) — reciprocity, commitment, social proof, authority, liking, scarcity — are also a scam operator's playbook. The same cognitive shortcuts that make human cooperation possible make human exploitation possible.[^li3-1]

> **[FAIRNESS]** Your Agent may describe common scam demographics in ways that reinforce stereotypes. Scam vulnerability correlates with cognitive load and isolation, not intelligence or age. Ask your Agent to focus on *patterns of approach* rather than *profiles of victims.*

<!-- RESEARCH NEEDED (HUMAN CONDITION): The psychology of shame after fraud. The cultural response — "you should have known better" — punishes the victim for a quality we need more of, not less. This flips the cultural narrative and deserves a strong callout or meme. -->

#### Li-4: Planning Care for Aging
**Strategy:** Research + Navigate
**See also:** H-7: Preparing for End-of-Life Conversations, M-3: Financial Coach (for Social Security and inheritance planning), L-1: Negotiating a Contract (for care facility agreements)
**The Majordomo says:** *There is a period in most people's lives when the person who took care of everything becomes the person who needs care. The role reversal arrives without training, without a manual, and without the emotional infrastructure to handle it. The American system assumes families will figure it out. Other countries built eldercare systems. You have your Agent and the entries that follow.*
**The spec:**
```
I am [beginning to plan for / currently managing] care for
[my parent / relative / spouse] who is [age] and dealing with
[describe the situation: declining mobility, cognitive changes,
recent diagnosis, can no longer live alone, etc.].
My current situation:
— Where they live now: [with me / alone / assisted living / other]
— Their financial situation: [brief — pension, savings, Medicare,
   Medicaid, none of these]
— What help they currently have: [family, home aide, none]
— What is changing or has changed: [the specific trigger]
Please help me:
1. Understand what level of care they may need now and next
2. What programs or benefits they may qualify for
3. What legal documents should be in place if they aren't already
4. What decisions I should NOT delay
```
**What to do with the output:** Make a list of the time-sensitive items — power of attorney, healthcare proxy, benefit applications — and do those first. The emotional work is real but the paperwork has deadlines. See H-7 for the conversation itself.

> **[TIP]** *"My [parent] has [condition] and [Medicare/Medicaid/no insurance]. What is the single most important thing I should do this week that I am probably not doing?"* — Start with the gap you don't know about.

> **[FAIRNESS]** Eldercare resources vary enormously by state. Ask your Agent to be specific to your state and county. Federal programs are the floor, not the ceiling.

#### Li-5: Planning Trips
**Strategy:** Prepare + Navigate
**See also:** Li-10: Language Learning Partner, Tr-2: Public Transit and Cycling
**The Majordomo says:** *Travel planning has been split between two industries: the agencies that charge for expertise and the search engines that charge for attention. Both optimize for their revenue, not your experience. Your Agent has no advertising revenue, no partner hotels, and no reason to recommend the flight with the worst layover because it pays a higher affiliate commission.*
**The spec:**
```
I am planning a trip to [destination] for [number of people]
from [dates]. My budget is [range].
What matters most to me: [relaxation / adventure / culture /
food / seeing family / something specific]
Constraints: [kids' ages, mobility issues, dietary needs,
visa requirements, anxiety about flying, first time abroad, etc.]
Please help me:
1. Build a realistic itinerary that accounts for travel time
   and energy — not a highlight reel
2. Flag things that could go wrong and how to prepare for them
3. Tell me what I'm likely to overpay for and where to save
4. Give me the three things about this destination that most
   travelers wish they'd known before arriving
```
**What to do with the output:** Print the itinerary. Share it with everyone going. Ask your Agent for a packing list specific to the destination and season. Before you leave, ask: *"What documents do I need, and what happens if I lose them?"*

Your Agent eliminates the entertainment value and keeps the trip.

> **[TIP]** *"I have a 4-hour layover in [city]. What can I actually do with that time, and what's the latest I can leave the airport and still make my connection?"*

#### Li-7: Raising Kids
**Strategy:** Research + Navigate
**See also:** Li-1: Preparing for a Difficult Conversation (for the hard talks), Li-4: Planning Care for Aging (the other end of the caregiving spectrum), H-17: Talking to Your Kids About Drugs
**The Majordomo says:** *Every generation of parents is terrified they are doing it wrong. This is not neurosis — it is a rational response to the fact that the stakes are a human life and the research keeps changing. [Dr. Spock](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benjamin_Spock) told Boomers to trust their instincts, which was revolutionary. The current research says instincts are a good start but not enough. The billionaire class has nannies, tutors, and developmental consultants on retainer. Your Agent has read the same developmental psychology they cite.*
**The spec:**
```
I am a parent of a [age] child and I'm dealing with
[specific situation: behavioral issue, school decision,
developmental concern, family conflict, milestone question, etc.].
What I've tried: [if anything]
What I'm worried about: [the real fear, not the surface one]
My family situation: [relevant context — single parent, blended
family, co-parenting, grandparent involvement, etc.]
Please:
1. Tell me what the current research says about this
2. Give me 2-3 specific things I can do this week
3. Tell me what's normal vs. what might need professional attention
4. Be honest about what the research doesn't know yet
```
**What to do with the output:** Parenting advice from any source — including your Agent — should be filtered through your knowledge of your specific child. If the advice doesn't fit, say so: *"That doesn't match what I see with my kid. Here's what actually happens: [describe]. What else should I try?"* Your Agent adjusts. Books don't.

> **[MEME]** We used to change the channel by walking to the TV and watched reruns of children using washboards on [*Nick at Nite*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nick_at_Nite). Now smart homes automate everything and robots fold laundry in viral videos. The nature of work is changing. So should chores. The number one skill to teach a child is not any specific task — it is knowing when to ask for help and how to evaluate the answer.

> **[SCIENCE]** Baumrind's parenting styles framework (1966) — authoritative, authoritarian, permissive — remains the most replicated finding in developmental psychology, with authoritative parenting (high warmth, high structure) consistently producing the best outcomes across cultures and decades.[^li7-1]

> **[FAIRNESS]** Parenting research has historically oversampled white, middle-class, two-parent households. When your Agent generalizes about "what research shows," ask: *"Is this finding consistent across income levels and family structures?"* The answer is sometimes no.

<!-- RESEARCH NEEDED: Baumrind (1966) "Effects of Authoritative Parental Control on Child Behavior" is solid, but the cross-cultural replication is more nuanced. Chao (1994) challenged the framework's applicability to Chinese-American families. Deater-Deckard et al. (2011) on cultural moderation of discipline effects. The "authoritative is best" consensus is real but has documented boundary conditions. Need both citations. -->

<!-- RESEARCH NEEDED (HUMAN CONDITION): Parental guilt is the most universal and least researched emotion in the caregiving literature. Fathers report less guilt but more anxiety about inadequacy — possibly the same emotion, differently expressed and differently permitted. The entry should name this without pathologizing it. Nuclear family isolation is a historical anomaly, not a norm. Parents are doing alone what humans never evolved to do alone. The entry should acknowledge this structural reality — not as excuse but as context. You are not failing. You are attempting something unprecedented. Grandparents, neighbors, extended family — the support infrastructure that existed for most of human history has been disassembled by mobility, suburbanization, and economic pressure. -->

This is a place where the entry should model scientific humility — "the research says X, but the confidence level is lower than the headlines suggest." -->

<!-- RESEARCH NEEDED (HUMAN CONDITION): The school choice anxiety — public vs. private vs. charter vs. homeschool — is a class-inflected decision disguised as an educational one. <!-- RESEARCH NEEDED: Developmental milestones — the CDC updated milestone guidelines in 2022, shifting some markers later (first words, walking). <!-- RESEARCH NEEDED (HUMAN CONDITION): Tradition vs. modernity in child-rearing. Every culture passes down practices that were adaptive in their context and may or may not be adaptive now. Spanking is the clearest example: widely practiced, culturally defended, and contradicted by 50+ years of research (Gershoff & Grogan-Kaylor, 2016, meta-analysis of 75 studies). #### Li-8: Wellness Coach
**Strategy:** Research + Draft (Expert Role)
**See also:** H-8: Wellness and Nutrition Coach (for physical health), H-13: Understanding Addiction, H-1: Understanding a Diagnosis
**The Majordomo says:** *Mental health care in America has a median wait time of 48 days for a new patient appointment. A therapy session costs $100 to $250 out of pocket. The people who need help most — the isolated, the overwhelmed, the financially strained — face the highest barriers to getting it. Your Agent is not a therapist. It is, however, available at 2 AM when the therapist is not, and it can do several things a therapist would recommend you do between sessions.*
**The spec:**
```
Act as a supportive wellness coach. I am not in crisis —
I want to work on [specific goal: managing stress, building
a journaling habit, improving sleep, processing a difficult
period, developing coping strategies, understanding my patterns].
My situation: [relevant context]
What I've tried: [if anything]
What's getting in the way: [be honest — time, motivation,
shame, not knowing where to start]
Please:
1. Help me understand what might be going on
2. Suggest one specific practice I can start today
3. Tell me when this is something I should bring to
   a professional instead
```
**What to do with the output:** Start with the one thing. Not three things. One. If the Agent suggests journaling, write for five minutes tonight. If it suggests a breathing exercise, do it once. The compound effect of small consistent actions is better documented than the effect of ambitious plans that last three days.

> **[FAIRNESS]** Your Agent will recommend therapy when appropriate. If cost or access is the barrier, say so: *"I can't afford therapy right now. What can I do on my own that has the most evidence behind it?"* The answer exists and is better than nothing.

**Common wellness Expert Role prompts:**
```
Act as a cognitive behavioral coach. I keep having this thought
pattern: [describe the loop — catastrophizing, rumination,
all-or-nothing thinking, etc.].
Help me identify what's happening and give me a specific
technique to interrupt it.
```
```
Act as a journaling coach. I want to start journaling but
I don't know what to write about and I feel self-conscious.
Give me a simple framework I can use for 5-10 minutes a day
that doesn't require me to be a good writer.
```
```
Act as an occupational therapy coach. I am struggling with
[daily living task: morning routine, household organization,
time management, sensory overload, task initiation] and I want
practical strategies, not motivational advice.
```

<!-- RESEARCH NEEDED (HUMAN CONDITION): The stigma of seeking help is declining in surveys but persists in behavior. #### Li-9: Making a Household Budget That Holds
**Strategy:** Draft + Decide
**See also:** M-3: Financial Coach, Ho-6: Understanding Your Utility Bill
**The Majordomo says:** *Budgeting advice assumes the problem is discipline. For most households, the problem is math — income that does not cover expenses, no matter how disciplined the allocation. Your Agent will not lecture you about lattes. It will help you see where the money actually goes, identify the three largest levers you can pull, and build a plan that survives contact with reality.*
**The spec:**
```
I want to build a household budget that actually works.
My situation:
— Monthly income (after taxes): [amount]
— Number of people in household: [X]
— Biggest expenses I know about: [rent, car, childcare, debt, etc.]
— What I'm struggling with: [running out before payday /
   can't save anything / don't know where it goes / debt spiral /
   one emergency away from crisis]
Please:
1. Help me categorize my spending into needs, wants, and debts
2. Identify the 2-3 biggest levers I have — the places where
   a change would make the most difference
3. Build a realistic monthly plan that includes some flexibility
   — a plan I'll actually follow, not an ideal I'll abandon
4. Tell me honestly: is this a budgeting problem or an income problem?
```
**What to do with the output:** Track actual spending for one month before adjusting the budget. Most people are surprised by at least one category. Ask your Agent: *"Here's what I actually spent last month: [list]. How does this compare to the plan, and what's the one change that would have the biggest impact?"*

> **[SCIENCE]** Mullainathan & Shafir (2013) document "scarcity mindset" — financial stress reduces cognitive bandwidth by the equivalent of 13 IQ points, making financial decisions harder precisely when they matter most.[^li9-1]

#### Li-10: Language Learning Partner
**Strategy:** Research + Navigate (Expert Role)
**See also:** Li-5: Planning Trips (for travel-specific language prep)
**The Majordomo says:** *Language immersion programs cost thousands of dollars. [Duolingo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Duolingo) is free and teaches you how to say “the elephant drinks milk” in seventeen languages. Private tutors charge $40 to $80 per hour and are available when their schedule permits. Your Agent is available now, infinitely patient, never visibly disappointed when you conjugate the same verb wrong for the fifth time, and will have an actual conversation with you about the things you actually need to say — not the things a textbook thinks you should practice.*
**The spec:**
```
Act as a patient language tutor for [language].
My current level: [complete beginner / basic phrases / conversational]
My goal: [travel / family communication / work / general learning]
What I struggle with most: [pronunciation / grammar / vocabulary /
understanding native speakers]
How would you suggest we work together in our conversations?
```
**What to do with the output:** Start using it immediately. Language learning research consistently shows that production — trying to say things, getting them wrong, trying again — beats passive study. Your Agent can do something no textbook can: respond to what you say, correct you in real time, and adjust the difficulty to where you actually are rather than where a curriculum assumes you should be.


> **[TIP]** *”Pretend you’re a [waiter / taxi driver / shopkeeper / colleague] in [city] and only respond in [language]. If I get stuck, give me the phrase I need, then let me try again.”* — Role-play with your Agent is the closest thing to immersion without a plane ticket.

> **[SCIENCE]** Krashen’s [input hypothesis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Input_hypothesis) (1982) argues that language acquisition happens when learners receive input slightly above their current level — “i+1.” Your Agent can calibrate to exactly this level in real time, something classroom instruction cannot do for 25 students simultaneously. The evidence for comprehensible input as the primary driver of acquisition is among the most replicated findings in applied linguistics.[^li10-1]

> **[FAIRNESS]** Your Agent’s language ability varies by language. It is strongest in English, Mandarin, Spanish, French, German, and Japanese. For less-resourced languages — many Indigenous languages, regional dialects, signed languages — its accuracy drops significantly. If you are working in a less common language, verify key phrases with a native speaker or community resource. Your Agent will tell you when it is less confident if you ask.

#### Li-11: Escaping Social Media
**Strategy:** Research + Decide
**See also:** Li-7: Raising Kids (for the screen time conversation), Li-8: Wellness Coach (for compulsive behavior patterns)
**The Majordomo says:** *The discourse says “screen time.” The research says “social media.” These are not the same variable. Using the internet to learn how a carburetor works is a different neurological event than scrolling a feed engineered to make you feel slightly worse about yourself at intervals calibrated to keep you scrolling. One is the most capable self-education infrastructure ever built. The other is an engagement-optimization engine that colonized it. The intervention is not less internet. It is better internet.*

**The spec:**
```
I want to reduce my social media use [or help my kid reduce theirs].
My current situation:
— Platforms I use most: [list them]
— How much time I estimate I spend: [hours/day]
— What I get out of it: [connection, news, entertainment, habit, nothing]
— What it costs me: [time, mood, sleep, focus, comparison anxiety]
— What I’ve tried: [app limits, deleting apps, cold turkey, nothing]
Please help me:
1. Understand the difference between purposeful internet use
   and engagement-optimized consumption
2. Identify which of my current habits are serving me and which aren’t
3. Build a realistic reduction plan that doesn’t require
   willpower I don’t have — change the cues, not the person
4. Suggest what to replace the habit with — the gap needs filling
   or the habit comes back
```
**What to do with the output:** Start with one platform. The one that makes you feel worst, or the one that eats the most time. Remove it from your phone — not your life, just your pocket. You can still use it on a computer, which introduces enough friction to break the automatic-checking loop. The research is clear: the harm concentrates in compulsive mobile checking, not in deliberate use.

> **[SCIENCE]** A landmark study in the *American Economic Review* exploited Facebook’s staggered rollout across U.S. college campuses as a natural experiment. College-wide Facebook access caused a 7% increase in severe depression and a 20% increase in anxiety disorders — effects roughly one-fifth the magnitude of losing a job. The mechanism is not use itself but emotional dependency: routine use correlates with positive outcomes, while compulsive checking driven by fear of missing out correlates negatively across every measure.[^li11-1]

> **[SCIENCE]** The distinction between purposeful and passive internet use is not a value judgment — it is a measurable difference. Research on primary school students found that using the internet to search for information favors educational outcomes, while social network use hinders them. These are categorically different activities, not points on a single spectrum.[^li11-2]

> **[MEME]** The web evolved from text to graphics to audio to video to interactive simulation — mapping almost precisely onto the full range of human learning modalities. Then social media arrived and replaced “learn anything” with “react to everything.” The infrastructure is still there. The feeds are in the way. Moving the feeds is the project.

> **[FAIRNESS]** “Just delete it” is advice that ignores the people for whom social media is their primary connection to community — isolated caregivers, people with disabilities, immigrants maintaining ties to home. The goal is not abstinence. The goal is shifting the ratio from reactive to purposeful. Ask your Agent: *”I need social media for [specific reason]. Help me keep that function and reduce the rest.”*
