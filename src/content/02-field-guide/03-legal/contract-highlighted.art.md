Hand-drawn #2 pencil sketch of a single contract page on plain white
paper. Standard legal formatting --- numbered clauses, dense
paragraph blocks, the typewritten look of legal boilerplate. The
clause text is fictional and illustrative --- placeholder lorem-style
legal language, no real contract, no real party names.

Three sections marked in ballpoint pen:

- Red ballpoint circles around a penalty/one-sided clause
- Yellow ballpoint underline on an unusual or non-standard clause
- Green ballpoint checkmarks beside two standard provisions

A single page, lying flat. No staple, no folder, no desk surface, no
hand holding it. No highlighter swipes --- annotations are circles,
underlines, and checkmarks in pen, not blocks of yellow highlighter.

**No meta-elements --- non-negotiable.** The illustration contains
only the contract page and its pen markings. No color swatches,
palettes, legends, keys ("red = bad / green = ok"), hex codes,
callouts pointing at the page from outside, sidebar text, or any UI
explaining the colors or technique.

**Background: pure white, `#FFFFFF`, flat.** Not gray, not off-white,
not cream, not paper texture, not a notebook page. The build removes
white to create transparency, so any gray will show as a halo in the
ePub.

**Watch out for:**

- NO real company names, real signatures, or real party names in
  the body text
- NO yellow highlighter rectangles --- yellow is a pen underline,
  not a swipe
- NO ruled or graph-paper background bleeding through the page
- NO color legend or key in a corner of the image
