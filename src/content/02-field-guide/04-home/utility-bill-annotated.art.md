Hand-drawn #2 pencil sketch of a single-page utility bill, lying
flat on a pure white field. Standard residential bill layout ---
service address block, account number, billing period, a line-item
table of charges, a total due, a payment stub at the bottom. The
text and numbers are fictional and illustrative --- a placeholder
utility ("Sample Power & Light"), a made-up account number, dollar
amounts that are clearly example values, no real customer, no real
provider.

Key line items annotated in blue ballpoint pen:

- A circle around the **base service charge**, with a margin note:
  "fixed --- you pay this even at zero usage"
- An arrow pointing to the **usage tier rows**, margin note:
  "tiered --- price per kWh climbs with use"
- A circle around the **delivery / distribution charge**, margin
  note: "the wires, not the electrons"
- An arrow at the **taxes & fees block**, margin note: "city + state"

The annotations are in #2 pencil for the connecting arrows and blue
ballpoint pen for the circles and handwritten margin notes --- the
same hand as the rest of the book.

**No meta-elements --- non-negotiable.** The illustration contains
only the bill and its pen annotations. No color swatches, palettes,
legends, keys, hex codes, callouts from outside the page, sidebar
text, or any UI explaining the colors or technique. No "EXAMPLE"
watermark stamped across the bill.

**Background: pure white, `#FFFFFF`, flat.** Not gray, not off-white,
not cream, not paper texture, not a notebook page, not a kitchen
table. The bill is the only object; the field around it is pure
white. The build removes white to create transparency, so any gray
will show as a halo in the ePub.

**Watch out for:**

- NO real utility company logos (PG&E, ConEd, Duke, etc.)
- NO real customer names, real addresses, real account numbers
- NO highlighter rectangles --- annotations are pen circles and
  arrows
- NO graph paper or ruled-paper backing --- the bill prints on
  plain stock
- NO QR code or barcode rendered with realistic data
