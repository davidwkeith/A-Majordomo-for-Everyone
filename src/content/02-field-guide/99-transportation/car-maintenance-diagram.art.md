Hand-drawn #2 pencil side-profile diagram of a generic 4-door sedan,
drawn in the spirit of a Chilton repair-manual cutaway sketched from
memory. The car body is a clean line drawing --- doors, windows,
wheels, side mirrors --- no color fill, no shading except light
hatching where the manual style would put it (under the body, in
the wheel wells).

Six labeled arrows point to maintenance areas:

- **Engine bay (front):** three arrows, fanned out, pointing to
  *engine oil*, *coolant*, and *air filter*
- **Front wheels:** one arrow pointing to *brakes*
- **Rear area / trunk:** one arrow pointing to *battery* (it lives
  in the trunk on enough cars to be worth showing here)
- **All four tires (or the visible two):** one arrow pointing at a
  tire labeled *tires*

Each label is hand-lettered in small block capitals in #2 pencil,
the same hand as the rest of the book --- not a typeset font.
Arrows are pencil lines with small open arrowheads.

The car is fictional and generic --- a placeholder sedan silhouette,
not a recognizable Honda Accord, Toyota Camry, or Ford Taurus
specifically. No badges, no make/model name on the car.

**No meta-elements --- non-negotiable.** The illustration contains
only the car and its six labeled arrows. No color swatches,
palettes, legends, keys, hex codes, callout boxes outside the
diagram, sidebar text, or any UI explaining the technique. No
external "MAINTENANCE GUIDE" caption beyond the arrow labels
themselves. The arrow labels are the labels --- there is no second
layer of explanatory text.

**Background: pure white, `#FFFFFF`, flat.** Not gray, not off-white,
not cream, not paper texture, not a notebook page, not a garage
floor. The car is the only object; the field around it is pure
white. The build removes white to create transparency, so any gray
will show as a halo in the ePub.

**Watch out for:**

- NO real car make/model logo on the body
- NO graph paper, ruled paper, or notebook page underneath the
  diagram --- this is an inline graphic, not a chapter opener
- NO color fill on the car --- all pencil, no ballpoint
- NO mileage/maintenance interval table beside the car
- NO mechanic figure, hand, wrench, or tool overlaid on the diagram
