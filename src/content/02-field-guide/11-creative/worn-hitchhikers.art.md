Hand-drawn #2 pencil still life. Three drawn objects on a pure white
field, no surface or table line drawn beneath them.

**1. Mac Classic.** The compact all-in-one beige Macintosh from
1990, drawn with the same obsessive detail as the Trinitron. The
small CRT screen, the floppy slot below it, the flat front bezel,
the rainbow Apple logo, the keyboard cable exiting the back. On the
screen, a generic period word processor is open in a
single window, drawn at unhinged detail --- title bar, menu bar,
ruler, blinking cursor in the upper-left of the document --- but
the document body is **completely empty**. Nothing written. The
screen is the only place where the master-palette colors appear
(Charcoal window chrome, Recycled menu bar, Blue title bar accent,
Black cursor); the rest of the drawing is graphite only.

**2. Battered paperback of The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy.**
Resting in front of the keyboard. The classic cover --- recognizable
silhouette, "DON'T PANIC" reading legibly, but the cover art rendered
loosely so it does not reproduce a copyrighted illustration verbatim.
Creased spine, dog-eared corners, a coffee ring on the cover, the
kind of book that has been read four times and lent twice and come
back rougher. Sits at a slight angle.

**3. A #2 pencil**, drawn with obsessive detail and resting
diagonally across the book: hex barrel, ferrule crimp marks, an
eraser worn down at an angle from heavy use, the stamped "No. 2"
text, the sharpened tip showing exposed graphite and wood grain.

**Around the workspace,** a couple of small Post-It-note doodles ---
a tiny rocket ship, a falling whale, a flower pot, a question mark.
Idle margin sketches on yellow Post-Its (Yellow from the master
palette). They float as drawn objects on the white field, not on a
notebook page.

**No meta-elements --- non-negotiable.** The illustration contains
only the Mac, the book, the pencil, and the Post-Its. No color
swatches, palettes, legends, keys, hex codes, callouts pointing at
the objects, sidebar text, or any UI explaining the colors or
technique. No external caption.

**Background: pure white, `#FFFFFF`, flat.** Not gray, not off-white,
not cream, not paper texture, not a desk grain, not a notebook page.
The objects float on pure white. The build removes white to create
transparency, so any gray will show as a halo in the ePub.

**Watch out for:**

- NO college-ruled notebook paper anywhere in the frame --- the
  doodles are on Post-Its, not on a ruled page
- NO verbatim reproduction of the original UK paperback cover art
- NO chair, desk, lamp, mug, or other workspace objects beyond the
  list above
- NO text written on the Mac's screen --- the document is blank
- NO graph paper, no grid background
