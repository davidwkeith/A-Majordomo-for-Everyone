Hand-drawn #2 pencil sketch of a hand holding an original iPhone in
**portrait orientation**, camera app open. The phone is drawn with
the same obsessive technical detail as the Trinitron --- the
volume buttons on the left edge, the silent switch above them, the
single home button below the screen, the chrome bezel, the
skeuomorphic camera-app shutter button at the bottom of the screen,
the small thumbnail of the last shot in the corner.

**Orientation --- non-negotiable.**

- **Portrait, not landscape.** The phone is taller than it is wide.
- **Home button on the bottom short edge,** centered.
- **Front-facing camera dot on the top short edge,** centered.

If the phone ends up landscape, square, or with the home button on
a long edge, the image is wrong.

The thumb hovers over the on-screen shutter button --- not pressing
it, ready to snap. The hand holding the phone is slightly stiff
(people are the weak point); only the thumb and index-finger edge
need to read clearly.

**On the screen,** in 8-bit pixel art: the iconic 1967 Patterson-
Gimlin Bigfoot frame --- a dark figure walking away through a forest
clearing, turning to look back over its shoulder. Rendered at
phone-screen resolution but recognizable. Use 4--8 colors from the
master palette: Brown and Dark Green for the forest, Black and
Charcoal for the figure's silhouette, Skin Mid for forest-floor
warmth, Recycled for any sky/clearing highlights.

**True pixel grid (screen only) --- non-negotiable.** The Bigfoot
frame on the screen is actual 8-bit pixel art, not a blocky
illustration. Render on a literal uniform grid of axis-aligned
square pixels, as if captured from an NES framebuffer and enlarged
with nearest-neighbor scaling. Every pixel the same size, every
pixel a single flat color from the palette. Hard edges, no
anti-aliasing, no smooth curves, no airbrush, no pencil shading or
hatching inside the pixel image. The viewer should be able to count
individual pixels along an edge. The pencil drawing of the phone and
hand does not obey this rule --- only the screen image does.

**No meta-elements --- non-negotiable.** No "iPhone" wordmark, no
camera-app brand label, no caption inside the image, no labels
pointing at the buttons, no color swatches, no hex codes, no UI
chrome beyond the camera app's own visible controls.

**Background: pure white, `#FFFFFF`, flat.** Not gray, not off-white,
not cream, not paper texture, not a notebook page, not a forest
scene around the hand. Only the screen contains forest --- the field
around the phone-and-hand is pure white. The build removes white to
create transparency, so any gray will show as a halo in the ePub.

**Watch out for:**

- NO real forest behind the phone --- the only forest is on the
  screen
- NO modern iPhone (no notch, no Dynamic Island, no triple-camera
  bump) --- this is the original iPhone's body
- NO pinch-to-zoom UI gesture overlay, no AR reticle
- NO graph paper or ruled-paper backdrop
