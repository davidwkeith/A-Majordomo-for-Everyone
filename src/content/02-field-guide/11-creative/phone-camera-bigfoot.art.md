Hand-drawn #2 pencil sketch of a hand holding an original iPhone in portrait orientation, camera app open. On the screen: the iconic 1967 Patterson-Gimlin Bigfoot frame — the dark figure walking away, turning to look back, in a forest clearing. Rendered at phone-screen resolution but recognizable. The thumb hovers over the shutter button. The phone is drawn with the same obsessive detail as the Trinitron, the volume buttons, the skeuomorphic UX. The hand holding it is slightly stiff (people are the weak point).

The joke: the most famous photograph of the 20th century was shot on a rented 16mm camera by a cowboy who had no idea what he was doing. The framing is terrible. The subject is blurry. It is still the most analyzed footage in history. Composition matters less than pointing the camera at the right thing.
