Hand-drawn #2 pencil sketch of a small thermal-printed queue ticket,
the kind a DMV or county clerk's deli-counter dispenser spits out.
Two views of the same ticket, side by side, lying flat:

**Front (left view):** The ticket reads **"NOW SERVING"** at the top
in small block letters, then the queue number **`847`** in large
sans-serif type filling most of the ticket. Below the number, a
fictional placeholder agency line ("DEPT. OF MOTOR VEHICLES ---
SAMPLE BRANCH") and a tiny date/time stamp. Slightly crumpled edges
on one side, the way thermal stock curls.

**Back (right view):** The unprinted reverse side of the same
ticket, with a handwritten ballpoint-pen checklist of required
documents:

- ID
- Proof of address
- Payment

Each item has a small pencil checkbox beside it, two of three
checked in pen.

The two views float as drawn objects --- no counter, no hand, no
desk, no other tickets behind them.

**No meta-elements --- non-negotiable.** The illustration contains
only the two views of the ticket. No color swatches, palettes,
legends, keys, hex codes, callouts pointing at the views, sidebar
text labeling them "FRONT" and "BACK," or any UI explaining the
technique. No external "QUEUE TICKET" caption.

**Background: pure white, `#FFFFFF`, flat.** Not gray, not off-white,
not cream, not paper texture, not a counter, not a notebook page.
The tickets are the only objects; the field around them is pure
white. The build removes white to create transparency, so any gray
will show as a halo in the ePub.

**Watch out for:**

- NO real DMV branch name or city
- NO real agency seal or state crest
- NO barcode or QR code rendered with realistic data --- a tiny
  abstract barcode is fine, but it should not scan
- NO graph paper or ruled-paper backdrop
