## Strategy 5: Decide

*Map your options when you're at a fork.*

---

<!-- art: trinitron-s5-decide -->

*[Friends:S3E15 "The One Where Ross and Rachel Take a Break"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_One_Where_Ross_and_Rachel_Take_a_Break), 1997 — The spec was ambiguous. He executed anyway. This is the entire problem.*

---

**The episode:** Ross and Rachel have a fight. Rachel says she needs "a break." Ross interprets this as a breakup. Rachel does not. Ross acts on his interpretation within hours. Rachel discovers this the following morning. The ensuing dispute runs for three seasons of television because the original decision — what "a break" meant, what each party was agreeing to — was never confirmed before either of them acted on it.

It is one of the most famous unresolved disputes in 90s television. It was entirely preventable. One question — *"when you say 'a break,' do you mean we're broken up?"* — asked before anyone did anything, would have resolved it in thirty seconds.

**The lesson:** A decision with an ambiguous spec produces an outcome nobody wanted. The AI's job in this strategy is not to make the decision for you. It is to surface the options, name the tradeoffs, and identify the assumptions you're making before you act — so you don't spend the next three seasons arguing about what you meant.

*"Your Agent does not know your life. It should not tell you what to do. What it can do is lay out your options with their actual tradeoffs, in plain language, without the agenda that every human advisor carries."*

---

Your Agent does not know your life. It should not tell you what to do. What it can do is lay out your options with their actual tradeoffs, in plain language, without the agenda that every human advisor carries. The financial advisor who recommends a product has a commission structure. The contractor who says you need a full replacement has a margin on materials. Your Agent has neither. It will give you the map. You drive.

This is where the library meets the life. The AI can tell you the five-year cost difference between refinancing and staying put. It cannot tell you whether stability matters more to you than savings right now. It can lay out the medical options with their success rates. It cannot tell you how much risk your family can absorb this year. Those decisions live in your gut — the compressed wisdom of every hard choice you have already made, every person you are responsible for, every value you have tested against reality. That is not a feeling that occasionally overrides logic. It is the most expensive data you own, paid for in years of being alive. The AI has the library. You have the scar tissue. The scar tissue decides.

**What Decide is for:** Any situation where you're choosing between options and you want to understand the real tradeoffs before you commit.

**The core spec pattern:**

```
I'm trying to decide between [option A] and [option B].
My situation is [specific context].
The factors that matter most to me are [list].
Please give me the genuine tradeoffs of each option —
not a recommendation, just the honest analysis.
```

> **[SCIENCE]** Decision fatigue — the degradation of decision quality after a sustained period of choices — is one of the most replicated findings in behavioral economics (Baumeister et al., 1998; Danziger et al., 2011). Offloading the *analysis* to your Agent does not require willpower. It restores the cognitive resources needed for the final judgment call, which remains entirely yours.[^s5-1]

[^s5-1]: Baumeister, R.F. et al. (1998). "Ego depletion: Is the active self a limited resource?" *JPSP*, 74(5), 1252-1265. Danziger, S. et al. (2011). "Extraneous factors in judicial decisions." *PNAS*, 108(17), 6889-6892.

**Worked examples:** Repair vs. replace an appliance. Roth vs. traditional IRA. Escalate vs. settle a landlord dispute.
