TRINITRON CHAPTER OPENER. Roseanne and Dan at the kitchen table,
papers and bills spread between them, both leaning over the table.
Their expressions are the specific exhausted focus of people trying
to make a good decision from bad information. Coffee cups. A pen.
A contract or form neither of them quite understands. Paper angle:
determined by Roseanne and Dan's forward lean over table — roughly
15-20 degrees forward. Blue lines extend their lean beyond the
frame. Obsession zone: the top surface of the cabinet — the flat
shelf where the remote sits, drawn with obsessive attention to the
subtle plastic texture, the faint heat-warp from years of use, the
dust that settles in the ventilation seam where the top meets the
back panel, the tiny scratches from things placed and removed a
thousand times. The surface nobody looks at, rendered as if it
were the most important face of the object.

>> ROSEANNE: I read enough of it.
>> DAN: How much is enough?
