## Strategy 4: Navigate

*Find the path through a multi-step process you have never done before.*

---

<!-- art: trinitron-s4-navigate -->

*[Seinfeld:S4E20 "The Junior Mint"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Junior_Mint), 1993 — The correct procedure existed. Step one was "tell someone." Nobody took step one.*

---

**The episode:** Jerry and Kramer end up in a hospital observation gallery, watching a surgical procedure. Kramer, leaning too far over the railing, accidentally drops a Junior Mint into the open body cavity. The correct process in this situation is immediate and obvious: tell the surgeon. Instead, they say nothing. They hope the mint will be absorbed. The patient nearly dies. Then, improbably, recovers — the surgeon later credits the mint with introducing a life-saving element into the wound.

The recovery is luck. The navigation failure was a choice. The process existed. Step one was "tell someone immediately." Nobody took step one, and the subsequent steps — monitoring the situation, hoping for the best, not telling anyone — were an improvised sequence invented in the absence of the correct one.

**The lesson:** Large institutions rely on the fact that most people have never filed a small claims case, appealed an insurance denial, or contested a property tax assessment before. The process is not secret. It is just nowhere near you. The AI has read it and will give you the steps in the correct order, with the deadlines flagged, from wherever you currently are. You do not have to invent the sequence. You just have to follow it.

*"Large institutions rely on the fact that most people have never filed a small claims case, appealed an insurance denial, requested a FOIA document, or contested a property tax assessment before. The process is not secret. It is just nowhere near you."*

---

**What Navigate is for:** Any multi-step process you've never done before where the sequence matters and missing a step has consequences.

**The core spec pattern:**

```
I need to [accomplish specific goal] through [institution/system].
I have never done this before.
My current situation is [where you are in the process, if anywhere].
Please give me numbered steps in the exact order I need to do them,
including any deadlines or time-sensitive elements I should know about.
```

> **[MEME]** ["It's a Unix system. I know this."](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jurassic_Park_(film)) The Jurassic Park scene works because Lex has just enough knowledge of the interface to navigate a system she's never seen before and get out alive. Your Agent is the Unix system. This book is the thirty seconds of orientation.

**Worked examples:** Filing a first-level insurance appeal. Contesting a property tax assessment. Filing a small claims case.
