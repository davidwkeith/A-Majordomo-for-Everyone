## Strategy 7: Research

*Understand something before you commit to it.*

The commitment — the signature, the deposit, the appointment — comes with information asymmetry already baked in. The institution you're committing to knows everything about the terms. You know what they told you. Claude helps you close that gap before you sign anything or agree to anything or schedule anything you can't easily undo.

**What Research is for:** Any decision that involves a significant commitment of money, time, or health, where you want to understand the full picture before you're in it.

**The core spec pattern:**

```
I am considering [action/purchase/procedure/program].
I want to understand [specific questions] before I commit.
My situation is [relevant context].
Please give me what I need to know, flag anything I should
specifically ask about, and tell me what I should verify
independently before proceeding.
```

> **[SCIENCE]** Research on health literacy consistently identifies pre-decision information-seeking as a significant predictor of patient satisfaction and outcomes (Berkman et al., 2011). The same principle applies to financial and legal decisions: the quality of commitment correlates with the quality of pre-commitment information. Claude reduces the cost of that research to nearly zero.[^s7-1]

[^s7-1]: Berkman, N.D. et al. (2011). "Low health literacy and health outcomes: An updated systematic review." *Annals of Internal Medicine*, 155(2), 97-107.

**Worked examples:** Before a surgical procedure. Before signing a non-compete clause. Before choosing between two college financial aid offers.
