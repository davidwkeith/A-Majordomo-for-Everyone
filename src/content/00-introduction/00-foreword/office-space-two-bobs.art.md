The interview scene from Office Space, rendered in 8-bit pixel art
colored in ballpoint pen. A small windowless conference room seen
straight-on. On one side of the table, a single figure (Peter energy)
seated, slightly slouched, an open manila file folder in front of him.
On the other side, two figures in matching dark suits (the two Bobs)
--- one writing on a yellow legal pad, one gesturing mid-question with
a pen. Coffee cups on the table. The only window is an interior pane
in the back wall looking out onto the cubicle floor --- the silhouettes
of cubicle dividers visible through it. Fluorescent ceiling fixtures
above. No exterior view, no daylight. 8 to 10 colors from the master
palette, characters recognizable by silhouette and posture, not faces.
The scene is the moment of being asked to explain yourself inside a
system you cannot see.

**True pixel grid --- non-negotiable.** This is actual 8-bit pixel
art, not a blocky illustration. Render on a literal uniform grid of
axis-aligned square pixels, as if captured from an NES or SNES
framebuffer and enlarged with nearest-neighbor scaling. Every pixel
the same size, every pixel a single flat color from the palette.
Hard edges between colors, no anti-aliasing, no smooth curves, no
airbrush, no sub-pixel detail. Diagonals are stair-stepped. The
viewer should be able to count pixels along an edge.

**No meta-elements --- non-negotiable.** The illustration contains
only the subject matter --- the conference room, the table, the
people, the interior window. No color swatches, palettes, legends,
keys, hex codes, callouts, arrows pointing at parts of the drawing,
sidebar text, or any UI explaining the colors or technique. No
caption text inside the image (no "Office Space," no character
names). The drawing is a finished artifact, not a draft with
technical notes.
