Picard on the Enterprise bridge, the "the line must be drawn here"
moment, rendered in 8-bit pixel art colored in ballpoint pen.
Picard in profile or three-quarter view, one arm extended forward
in the commanding gesture. Bridge consoles and crew silhouettes
in the background. Pure white background. NES-era palette from the
master palette, 6-8 colors max. Uniform red (palette Red), skin tone
(Skin Light), hair (Warm Gray), bridge consoles (Navy, Charcoal).
The posture carries the scene --- the moment of assertion.

**True pixel grid --- non-negotiable.** This is actual 8-bit pixel
art, not a blocky illustration. Render on a literal uniform grid of
axis-aligned square pixels, as if captured from an NES framebuffer
and enlarged. Every pixel the same size. Hard edges between colors,
no anti-aliasing, no smooth curves, no airbrush, no sub-pixel
detail. Diagonals are stair-stepped. The viewer should be able to
count pixels along an edge. Reference: Mega Man, Final Fantasy,
Star Trek 25th Anniversary on NES --- not a modern illustrator's
"retro" style.
