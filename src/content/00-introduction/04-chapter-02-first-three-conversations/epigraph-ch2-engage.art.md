Enterprise-D bridge viewscreen in 8-bit pixel art, approximately 48x32 pixels scaled up. The viewscreen shows stars elongating into warp streaks. The frame of the viewscreen is visible --- the distinctive oval shape of the Enterprise-D main viewer. 3-color palette from the master palette: Black (viewscreen interior), Recycled (stars and bridge wall behind the viewscreen), Warm Gray (viewscreen frame). Pure white background outside the image area.

**True pixel grid --- non-negotiable.** This is actual 8-bit pixel
art, not a drawing on graph paper. Render on a literal uniform grid
of axis-aligned square pixels --- as if captured from an NES
framebuffer at ~48x32 and then enlarged with nearest-neighbor
scaling. Every pixel the same size, every pixel a single flat color
from the 4-color palette. Hard edges, no anti-aliasing, no smooth
curves, no airbrush, no pencil shading, no graph-paper grid lines
showing through. Diagonals are stair-stepped. The oval viewscreen
frame is approximated by stair-stepped pixel jumps, not a smooth
curve. The warp streaks are runs of horizontal pixels, not
hand-drawn lines. The viewer should be able to count individual
pixels along any edge. Reference: Star Trek 25th Anniversary on NES,
or any NES space-shooter title screen --- not a drawing in a
notebook.
