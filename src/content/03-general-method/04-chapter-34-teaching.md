## Chapter 34: Teaching This to Someone You Love

*(Full chapter text to be drafted per outline.)*

The core technique: do not explain it. Do it together, once, on a problem they already have. The explanation comes after the first result, not before.

> **[SCIENCE]** Social learning theory (Bandura, 1977) consistently shows that observational learning -- watching someone else successfully complete a task -- is a more powerful driver of behavior adoption than instruction alone. "Watch me do this with my insurance bill" is a more effective introduction to AI tools than any explanation, including this book.[^34-1]

[^34-1]: Bandura, A. (1977). *Social Learning Theory.* Prentice Hall. Self-efficacy -- the belief that one can successfully perform a behavior -- is the strongest predictor of whether a person will attempt a new technology (Compeau & Higgins, 1995). Self-efficacy is built through observation and small wins, not through reading manuals.
