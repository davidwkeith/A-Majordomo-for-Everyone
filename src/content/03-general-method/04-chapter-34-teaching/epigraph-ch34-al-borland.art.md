Al Borland in 8-bit pixel art, approximately 32x48 pixels scaled up.
Standing pose with flannel shirt and beard. Holding a clipboard at
chest height --- the specification document Tim never reads. Patient,
competent expression. 4-color palette drawn from the master palette:
Red and Maroon for the flannel plaid, Skin Light for face, Brown for
beard and hair, Recycled for the clipboard paper, Charcoal for the
clipboard backing and dark plaid lines. Colored in ballpoint pen.

**True pixel grid --- non-negotiable.** This is actual 8-bit pixel
art, not a blocky illustration. Render on a literal uniform grid of
axis-aligned square pixels, as if captured from an NES framebuffer
and enlarged with nearest-neighbor scaling. Every pixel the same
size, every pixel a single flat color from the palette. Hard edges
between colors, no anti-aliasing, no smooth curves, no airbrush, no
sub-pixel detail. Diagonals are stair-stepped. The viewer should be
able to count pixels along an edge. Reference: Mega Man, Final
Fantasy IV, Link's Awakening --- not a modern illustrator's "retro"
style.

**No meta-elements --- non-negotiable.** The illustration contains
only Al Borland. No color swatches, palettes, legends, keys, hex
codes, callouts, arrows pointing at the figure, sidebar text, or any
UI explaining the colors or technique. No caption text inside the
image (no "Al Borland," no "Tool Time," no character name label).
The drawing is a finished sprite, not a draft with technical notes.

**Background: pure white, `#FFFFFF`, flat.** Not gray, not off-white,
not cream, not paper texture, not a notebook page. The build removes
white to create transparency, so any gray will show as a halo in the
ePub.

**Watch out for:**

- NO clipboard text content visible at this resolution --- the
  clipboard is a colored shape, not a readable document
- NO Tim Allen / Tim Taylor figure beside him --- Al stands alone
- NO "Tool Time" set, workshop background, or stage lights
- NO tool belt, hammer, or held tools other than the clipboard
- NO speech bubble, thought bubble, or dialogue text
