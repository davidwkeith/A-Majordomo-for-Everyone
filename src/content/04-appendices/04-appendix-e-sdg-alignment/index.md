## Appendix E: Alignment with UN Sustainable Development Goals

The Roddenberry Foundation's 2024 Prize referenced the UN Sustainable Development Goals as a framework for evaluating AI's benefit to society. The entries in this book map to the following SDGs:

| SDG | Relevant Entries in This Book |
|---|---|
| **SDG 1** No Poverty | #26 (cost of debt), #28 (severance), #74-79 (consumer protection), #109 (weatherization assistance) |
| **SDG 3** Good Health and Well-Being | #1-14 (all health chapters), #69-73 (food safety and nutrition) |
| **SDG 4** Quality Education | #69, #73, #75, #89-91, #104 (adult education) |
| **SDG 5** Gender Equality | #103 (discrimination complaints), #51-60 (family and relationships) |
| **SDG 7** Affordable and Clean Energy | #104-110 (Chapter 35, home and planet) |
| **SDG 10** Reduced Inequalities | #29-40 (legal access), #80-83, #102-103 (civil rights and civic life) |
| **SDG 11** Sustainable Cities and Communities | #44-50 (home and property), #80-83 (civic participation) |
| **SDG 13** Climate Action | #104-110 (Chapter 35) |
| **SDG 16** Peace, Justice, and Strong Institutions | #29-40 (legal), #80-83, #92-96, #102-103 (civic and rights) |
| **SDG 17** Partnerships for the Goals | Chapter 34 (teaching this to someone you love -- network effects of access) |
